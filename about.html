The short version

Every role I've had has followed the same pattern: I find a process that wastes people's time and I build something to fix it. At a call centre in Barcelona, that was a Python script to automate end-of-shift reporting. At Virgin Media O2, it was reverse-engineering Unica's alert system to give 13 people a clipboard in an environment that didn't have one. At Volkswagen Group, it was an Excel dashboard with live SQL connections to replace a spreadsheet nobody was filling in.

Now I'm a Senior CRM Analyst at Nationwide, designing campaigns that reach millions of customers. But the pattern continues — I've built internal tools, redesigned documentation processes, and outside of work I'm running AI-powered automation pipelines on a self-hosted Raspberry Pi. The transition to AI and business automation isn't a career change. It's the thing I've always done, with better tools.
